About MARËIS

Our story, our values, our commitment to the oceans

Our story

Born from a passion for our oceans

MARËIS was born in 2018 from the will of a group of passionate Guadeloupeans, lovers of the sea and concerned about the progressive degradation of our marine ecosystem. Divers, fishermen, biologists and ordinary citizens united around a common conviction: it was urgent to act to preserve the riches of our Caribbean waters.

The name "MARËIS" evokes both the sea (mar) and starfish (estrella), symbols of the fragile beauty of our marine environment. Since our creation, we have grown to become a community of over 150 active volunteers, united by the same commitment.

Today, MARËIS is recognized as a major player in marine protection in Guadeloupe, intervening both in the field and in schools to raise awareness among new generations.

The context

An ecosystem in danger

Water warming

The temperature of Caribbean waters is increasing every year, causing massive bleaching of corals. In Guadeloupe, nearly 50% of coral reefs are threatened.

Plastic pollution

Every year, tons of plastic waste invade our coasts and seabeds, threatening marine fauna and destroying natural habitats.

Threatened species

Sea turtles, manatees and many fish species are seeing their populations decline due to human activities and climate change.

Our values

What guides our action

01

Respect for life

We believe that all species have the right to exist. Our actions aim to preserve marine biodiversity in all its forms.

02

Scientific approach

We base our initiatives on rigorous scientific research and expert advice. Knowledge guides our actions.

03

Community engagement

Real change comes from collective commitment. We involve local communities, young people and businesses in our actions.

04

Intergenerational responsibility

We work for future generations. Every action taken today is a gift to tomorrow's world.

Acting for a sustainable future

We believe in the power of collective action. Our beach clean-up operations bring together hundreds of volunteers each year. We also work with local elected officials to promote coastal protection policies.
